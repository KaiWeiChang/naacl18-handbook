Abstract#==#This paper presents two novel datasets and a random-forest classifier to automatically predict literal vs. non-literal language usage for a high-frequent type of multi-word expression in a low-resource language, i.e., Estonian. We demonstrate the value of language-specific indicators induced from theoretical linguistic research, which outperform a high majority baseline when combined with language-independent features of non-literal language (e.g., abstractness).
